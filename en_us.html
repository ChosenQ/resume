Introduction:

As a fourth-year undergraduate studying in the School of physics and Astronomy at SYSU, I have a strong interest in leveraging cutting-edge technologies to solve real-world problems. Throughout my academic journey, I have acquired a broad range of technical skills and knowledge, among which I find Machine Leaning to be particularly intriguing and promising. My passion for this field has led me to acquire much of my Machine Learning expertise through online courses, such as those offered by YouTube, Coursera, and bilibili, as well as through research practices. With my solid foundation in Physics, Mathematics, Computer Science, and Machine Learning, I have successfully undertaken various research projects. Among these, the most noteworthy is my work with Professor Prof. Nicola R. Napolitano, wherein we developed a variety of models for galaxy images analysis. Specifically, our project utilized Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to perform structural decomposition in 2-component galaxies, culminating in the co-authorship of my first academic paper on the subject. As I look to the future, I am excited to continue expanding my expertise in Artificial Intelligence and participating in research endeavors focused on Reinforcement Learning, Multi-modal Analysis (e.g., image, video, text), and Interactive Intelligent Technologies. I am committed to harnessing these cutting-edge tools to drive actual contributions towards solving real-world challenges.
